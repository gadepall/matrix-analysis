\section*{Class 11, Chapter 11, Exercise 1.7}

Q. Find the centre and radius of the circle $x^2+y^2-4x-8y-45=0$.  

\solution
The equation of the circle in vector form is given by
\begin{align}
	\label{eq:circle} 
	\norm{\vec{x}}^2 + 2\vec{u}^{\top}\vec{x} + f = 0
\end{align}

where
\begin{align}
	\vec{u} &= -\vec{c}\\
	      f &= \norm{\vec{c}}^2 - r^2
\end{align}

The given circle is
\begin{align}
    \label{eq:given} 
    \norm{\vec{x}}^2  + 2\myvec{-2 & -4}\vec{x} - 45 = 0
\end{align}


Comparing the equations \eqref{eq:circle} and \eqref{eq:given}, we get
\begin{align}	
	\vec{u} &= \myvec{-2\\-4}\\
	\implies \vec{c} &= \myvec{2\\4}\\
	f &= \norm{\vec{c}}^2 - r^2\\
	\implies -45 &= \brak{\sqrt{2^2 + 4^2}}^2 - r^2\\
	\implies r^2 &= 65\\
	\implies r &= \sqrt{65}	
\end{align}
